Cold Email Isn’t Dead

Cold email is not dead. Generic, poorly targeted and operationally weak cold email is simply easier to ignore—and easier for inboxes to filter.

Deliverability7 min readSimon Lecat19 July 2026

Every year, someone announces the end of cold email. Response rates are lower. Inboxes are crowded. Filters are stricter. Buyers are more sceptical. All of that can be true without making the channel obsolete.

Cold email still works because business problems still exist and relevant conversations still begin before a formal buying process. What has changed is the standard required to earn attention.

The channel is not the strategy. Cold email performs when it is one disciplined layer inside a credible outbound system.

Why cold email feels harder

Sending has become easier, so more companies do it badly. Automation has increased volume faster than relevance. Buyers recognize generic templates immediately. Inbox providers have improved their ability to identify risky or unwanted sending patterns.

The result is not the death of email. It is a higher cost for weak execution.

Deliverability comes before copy

A message cannot create a conversation if it never reaches the primary inbox. Domain reputation, authentication, sending patterns, list quality and complaint rates all influence placement.

Teams often test subject lines while ignoring structural issues. That produces false conclusions because the copy is being judged on incomplete delivery. A sustainable programme protects the infrastructure, increases volume gradually and treats bounce and complaint signals seriously.

Targeting does most of the personalization

Personalization is frequently reduced to inserting a recent post, company milestone or first name. Those details can help, but they do not replace commercial relevance.

The strongest form of personalization is selecting a company because a specific problem is likely to matter, contacting the person who owns that problem and writing from that context. A relevant message with limited decoration usually outperforms an elaborate message sent to the wrong audience.

Relevance is more important than cleverness

Cold email copy does not need to be entertaining. It needs to be clear. The prospect should quickly understand why they were contacted, what situation the sender may help with and what the next step requires.

Overwritten messages create friction. Aggressive claims reduce trust. Artificial familiarity feels manipulative. Strong messages are concise, specific and honest about the reason for contact.

Volume should follow evidence

High volume is attractive because it creates visible activity. It also magnifies weak targeting and damages infrastructure faster. Start with a focused segment and a clear hypothesis. Review actual replies, not only rates. Improve the account selection and message before expanding.

Volume becomes useful when the system has something worth repeating.

Replies need an operating process

Positive replies lose value when responses are slow, qualification is unclear or scheduling creates friction. Negative replies are wasted when objections are not categorized. Out-of-office messages and referrals can also contain useful routing information.

Cold email performance therefore depends on what happens after the email. Reply handling is part of the channel.

Use email with other channels

Email can introduce the hypothesis. LinkedIn can create familiarity or provide context. Phone can clarify whether the problem exists and whether timing is relevant. The channels should reinforce one another rather than duplicate the same pitch.

A coordinated sequence also reduces the pressure placed on any single email to create the entire outcome.

Measure commercial quality

Open rates are increasingly unreliable and rarely indicate value. Track positive replies, qualified conversations, attended meetings and opportunities. Review performance by segment, role, message and sending environment.

A smaller campaign that creates the right conversations is more successful than a large campaign that produces activity without commercial movement.

What sustainable cold email looks like

Sustainable cold email uses healthy infrastructure, validated data, narrow targeting, relevant messaging, conservative sending patterns and disciplined follow-up. It learns from replies and protects reputation over time.

That is less dramatic than a growth hack. It is also far more durable.

Cold email is alive. Indiscriminate sending is what no longer deserves to survive.
